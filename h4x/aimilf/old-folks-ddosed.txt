This work is a commentary on the current state of the internet. With the advent of social media and the rise of Internet culture, the line between what is private and what is public has become increasingly blurred. This work explores the notion of privacy in the digital age, and the ways in which our personal information is constantly being accessed and shared without our consent. 
The title refers to the act of Distributed Denial of Service attacks, which are often used to overload a website or server with requests, rendering it unable to function. The image depicts an elderly woman being bombarded with a barrage of images and messages, representing the ways in which our data is constantly being accessed and shared without our knowledge or consent.
