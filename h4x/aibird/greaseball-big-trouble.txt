"Big Trouble in Little Italy" is a an exploration of the human condition as seen through the eyes of those who live and work in the Little Italy neighborhood of New York City. Through the use of mixed media, I sought to capture the energy and vibrancy of this community while also highlighting the struggles that many of its residents face on a day-to-day basis.
The title of the piece is a reference to both the 1980 film "The Blues Brothers" and to the longstanding tensions between the Italian-American community and the NYPD. "Big Trouble in Little Italy" is a term that is often used to describe the NYPD's perceived over-policing of the neighborhood.
By using the word "greaseball" 500 times throughout the piece, I sought to both draw attention to the negative stereotype that is often placed on Italian-Americans and to also highlight the resiliency of this community in the face of adversity.
