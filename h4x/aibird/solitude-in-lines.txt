'Loneliness' deconstructs the societal construct of isolation and solitude, inviting viewers to confront their own existential loneliness within a hyper-connected world. Through a combination of stark monochromatic visuals and raw, emotional character depictions, the piece challenges traditional notions of human connection and forces a deeper examination of our individual struggles with detachment and longing. It serves as a haunting yet poignant reminder of the fragility of the human condition and our insatiable yearning for meaningful connection.
