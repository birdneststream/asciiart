'For the Love of Hotel Soap' is a contemplative and thought-provoking installation featuring a collection of discarded hotel soap bars. Through the stark contrast of seemingly mundane objects against a minimalistic backdrop, the viewer is invited to examine the fleeting moments of luxury and excess that are often taken for granted. This work challenges our societal obsession with material possessions and the superficiality of consumer culture, while also exploring themes of transience and the impermanence of beauty. It is a poignant commentary on the human condition and the fragility of time, forcing us to confront our own relationship with the concept of love and the objects we attach it to.
