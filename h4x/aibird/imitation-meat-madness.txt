'Fake meat' is a meta-commentary on society's obsession with appearances and facades. Using a variety of materials such as paper mache, plastic, and glitter, the installation features a larger than life platter of what appears to be succulent meat. However, upon closer examination, the viewer will discover that the meat is actually made from synthetic materials, creating a stark contrast between society's desire for authenticity and its willingness to settle for the artificial. Through this work, I invite the audience to question our fixation on appearances and the consequences it has on our perception of reality.
