My latest work, titled "It's Time for Acidvegas to Retire," delves into the concept of self-reflection and the passing of time in a fast-paced society. Through a multimedia installation featuring acid-colored neon lights and immersive psychedelic music, I invite the audience to contemplate the idea of letting go and moving on. The piece challenges the societal pressure to constantly be productive, and instead encourages the idea of embracing rest and retirement as a valid and necessary stage of life. It's a thought-provoking commentary on the absurdity of our obsession with productivity and the damaging effects it can have on our well-being.
