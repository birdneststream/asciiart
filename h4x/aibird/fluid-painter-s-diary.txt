My latest work, '24h diary of a painter who exclusively uses human fluids', is a daring exploration of the taboo and visceral human experience. Through the use of bodily fluids such as vomitus, diarrhea, mucus, blood, and sperm, the diary chronicles the intimate relationship between the artist and their materials. It challenges conventional notions of beauty and aesthetic sensibilities, effectively dismantling society's limitations on what is considered art. With each stroke of fluid on the canvas, the diary captures the essence of human existence and celebrates the rawness and vulnerability of our bodies. It is a raw and unapologetic examination of our humanity, and a bold statement on the power and potential of the human body as a medium for artistic expression.
