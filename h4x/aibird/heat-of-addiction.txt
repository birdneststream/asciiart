My artistic masterpiece, 'Aluminum Pan of Heroin over Sterno Flame', aims to critique our societal obsession with instant gratification and the numbing effects of substance abuse.
By using the common household items of an aluminum pan and a sterno flame, I symbolize the accessibility and normalization of drug use in our society. The pan represents a vessel for the drug, while the sterno flame symbolizes the superficial and temporary high that numbs us from facing our deeper issues.
Through this piece, I am challenging viewers to reflect on the dangers and consequences of seeking quick fixes for our problems, and the potential devastation that can result from succumbing to the allure of instant gratification. It is a commentary on the destructive cycle of addiction and a call to action to address the root causes of our societal issues rather than masking them with substances.
