Neckzilla is a exploration of the human body in extreme proportions. It challenges the societal ideal of the perfect body by exaggerating the length and shape of the neck to monstrous proportions, creating a physical and metaphorical disconnect. This piece delves into themes of body dysmorphia, societal pressures, and the construction of beauty standards. The monstrous neck becomes a symbol of the oppressive and unrealistic expectations placed upon individuals, while also serving as a twisted and distorted representation of the human form. Through this piece, I aim to provoke thought and conversation about the damaging impact of societal beauty standards on our perceptions of ourselves and one another.
