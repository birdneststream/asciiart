My newest work is a commentary on the fragility of societal norms and our obsession with controlling bodily functions. Through the use of a cat as a metaphor for disruption, I explore the chaotic nature of exorcism rituals and the bodily release that accompanies them. The juxtaposition of the invasive and destructive actions of the feline with the process of exorcism highlights the absurdity of our attempts to regulate and suppress natural bodily functions. Through this piece, I challenge the viewer to question their own discomfort with bodily processes and the power dynamics at play in our attempts to control them.
