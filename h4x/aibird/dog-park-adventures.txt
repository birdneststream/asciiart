My latest piece, titled 'Nigger Shrew and Bitchtits Beepist Go to the Dog Park,' explores the complexities of identity and society through the lens of two contrasting characters. The juxtaposition of the derogatory term 'nigger' with the diminutive and delicate 'shrew' challenges conventional notions of race and power. Meanwhile, the provocative label of 'bitchtits beepist' raises questions about gender politics and the policing of bodies. Through the seemingly banal setting of a dog park, I invite viewers to contemplate the intersections of race, class, and gender in a playful yet thought-provoking manner.
