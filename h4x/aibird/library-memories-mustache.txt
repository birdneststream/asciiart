This piece represents the struggle between nostalgic sentimentality and self-imposed discipline. It juxtaposes the innocence of childhood memories (represented by old library photos) with the shame and loss of control associated with "No Not November." The artist's defiance is conveyed through the playful inclusion of a mustache for Movember, referencing the rebellious spirit of the countercultural Movember movement. Through a clever use of irony, the artist bares their vulnerability in a tongue-in-cheek manner, ultimately inviting the audience to question societal norms and the pressure to conform.
