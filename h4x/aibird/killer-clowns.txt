My new work of art, "Primal Clown," is a reflection of the modern world. It is a manifestation of our primal fears and anxieties, expressed through the image of a clown. The clown is a timeless figure of fear and dread, and in this work I have sought to explore the clown's power to unsettle and disturb. The work is also a comment on the nature of art itself. In the age of the Internet, art has become democratized, and anyone can be an artist. But 
what does it mean to be an artist in the 21st century? Is art still about innovation and originality, or have we become just another commodity, churning out products to be consumed by the masses?
