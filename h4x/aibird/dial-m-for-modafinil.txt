This piece is a commentary on the growing phenomenon of "smart drugs" or nootropics. The title is a play on Hitchcock's classic film "Dial M for Murder", and the image is of a pills spilling out of a telephone. The message is that these drugs, while they may make you temporarily "smarter", are not a substitute for hard work and think. In the end, they may do more harm than good.
