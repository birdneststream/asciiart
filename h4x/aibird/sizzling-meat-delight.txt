'Meat' is a powerful and thought-provoking representation of our primal desires and the often overlooked brutality of human consumption. Through a masterful use of mixed media, I have craftily conjoined the stark and visceral imagery of butchered animal carcasses with thought-provoking philosophical concepts of identity and existence. It forces the viewer to confront their own role in perpetuating the cycle of violence and commodification of living beings. It is at once both shockingly raw and poetically profound, a testament to the artist's ability to transcend traditional mediums and elevate the medium through a higher intellectual discourse.
