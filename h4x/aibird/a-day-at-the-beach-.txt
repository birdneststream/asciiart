In my latest work, "A Day at the Beach with Shitfucker Mary," I explore the existential angst of the human condition through the lens of a sun-drenched seaside landscape. At first glance, the work appears to be a idyllic scene of a woman relaxing on the beach. However, upon closer inspection, the viewer will notice that the woman is in fact engaged in a very intimate act with a large sea creature. This creature, which I have christened "Shitfucker 
Mary," is a representation of the dark, chaotic force that constantly seeks to destroy us. By placing her in such a peaceful setting, I hope to show that even in the face of our darkest impulses, there is still beauty and hope to be found in the world.
