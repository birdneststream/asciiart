My latest piece, 'Steal Master Will Not Come to 101', is a commentary on the fleeting nature of power and greed. The title alludes to a powerful figure, the 'Steal Master', who chooses not to visit a mundane location like 101. This juxtaposition highlights the potential downfall of those who prioritize material gain over human connections and meaningful experiences. The artwork itself consists of a distorted and abstract representation of the 'Steal Master', surrounded by mundane elements such as numbered rooms. By challenging conventional notions of power and placing it in a banal setting, I aim to provoke thought and spark discussions about the true nature of authority.
