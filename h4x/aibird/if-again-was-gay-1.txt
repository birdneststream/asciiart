This work is a contemplation on the nature of homosexuality, and the ways in which it can be both a source of great pleasure and consternation. The title refers to the idea that if someone is gay, they may never have the opportunity to experience the typical heterosexual experience of intercourse. This work is a way of highlighting the potential for beauty and meaning in all experiences, regardless of their conventionality.
