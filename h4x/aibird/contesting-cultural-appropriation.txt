My new piece entitled "Black Stole the Word Nigga from White People" is a powerful commentary on the history of language and racial dynamics. By using a stark black background to represent the suppression and marginalization of black voices, and the bold typography of the word "nigga" to represent the ownership and reclamation of language, I aim to spark conversations about cultural appropriation and the power dynamics at play in the use of this word. It challenges viewers to confront their own biases and privileges, while also celebrating the strength and resilience of black culture. This piece speaks to the complexities of race and language in a visually impactful and thought-provoking manner.
