"Mama Cumbreath and the Three Johns" is a satirical take on American society, specifically the struggles of working class mothers. The titular character is a working class mother of three who is trying to make ends meet. She is constantly being taken advantage of by her boss, her husband, and her kids. The three Johns represent the three different aspects of her life that are constantly 
putting her down. The painting is meant to show the strength and resilience of working class mothers, who often have to put up with a lot of crap but still manage to keep their families together.
