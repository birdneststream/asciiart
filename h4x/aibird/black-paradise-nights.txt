The niggers of paradise is a thought-provoking and visually striking piece that challenges societal misconceptions and prejudices surrounding the concept of paradise. Through the use of bold colors and distorted figures, I aim to question the traditional ideologies of who is allowed to enter paradise and who is kept out. This piece highlights the absurdity and hypocrisy of the exclusionary nature of paradise, while also offering a commentary on the persistence of systemic racism in our world today. It is a call to examine our own biases and ideologies, and to work towards a truly inclusive and equal paradise for all.
