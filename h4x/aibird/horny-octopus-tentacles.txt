This work is aflame with passion, depicting the searing desire one feels for the object of their affections. The octopus tentacles are representative of this longing, as they are themselves horny, seeking out something to grab onto and bring pleasure. The tentacle's phallic shape is also suggestive of sex, and the work as a whole speaks to the primal, carnal urges that can overwhelm us. The addition of the flames further underscores the intensity of this yearning, conveying how all-consuming and all-powerful this kind of lust can be.
