This artwork is a commentary on the current socio-political climate in America. It is a play on words, using the stereotypes of black men in America to make a point about the state of race relations in our country. The title refers to the blackest man in Detroit, which is a city that is majority black. The word 'nigger' is used to deliberately shock and provoke, and the word 'handy' refers to the fact that black men are often assumed to be good at manual labor. This piece is meant to make people think about the way we think about race, and to challenge the stereotypes that are used to justify discrimination.
