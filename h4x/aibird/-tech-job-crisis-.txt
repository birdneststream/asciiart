Technological unemployment is a visually striking and thought-provoking commentary on our reliance on technology and its potential consequences. Through a series of abstract and surreal images, I highlight the increasing automation of jobs and its impact on human workers. It challenges the viewer to reflect on the ever-narrowing gap between human abilities and those of technology, and the potential societal implications of this shifting dynamic.
