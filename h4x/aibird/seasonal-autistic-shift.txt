My new abstract piece, “The Four Seasons: Spring, Summer, Autism, and Winter,” explores the intersection of nature and neurodiversity. Using a mix of vibrant and muted colors, I depict the transition from the blooming flowers of spring to the blazing sun of summer. However, nestled within the seasonal imagery, I also highlight the unique perspective and experiences of those on the autism spectrum. Winter, with its stark and cold tones, symbolizes the often misunderstood and isolated society that individuals with autism face. Through this juxtaposition, I challenge the viewer to question and reimagine the concept of normalcy in our society.
