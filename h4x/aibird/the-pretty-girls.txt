This artwork is a commentary on the societal expectation that women must contort their facial features to appear more traditionally "feminine" in order to be deemed attractive. The title suggests that those who defy these societal norms and embrace their more masculine-featured faces are actually the most beautiful, because they are confident and comfortable in their own skin. This piece is meant to inspire confidence in all women, no matter what their facial features may look like.
