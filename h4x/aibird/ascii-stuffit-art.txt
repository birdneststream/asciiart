'Stuffit Expander' is a post-modern commentary on the digital age, exploring the dichotomy between the proliferation of technological advancements and the loss of tactile experience. Through a minimalist sculpture comprising of a metal box filled with various cords and wires, I invite the audience to question the ways in which we interact with our devices and the detritus left behind. By merging the mundane with the elaborate, 'Stuffit Expander' challenges our reliance on technology and the concept of tangible versus intangible. It is a thought-provoking exploration of our complex relationship with the digital world and the physical world.
