"Sushi Eating Contest" is a thought-provoking performance piece that challenges societal notions of competition and consumption. The installation features a group of individuals engaged in a fierce race to devour as much sushi as possible within a set time limit. Through this intense display, the audience is forced to confront their own relationship with excess and privilege. The raw and unapologetic nature of the piece leaves a lasting impression, stirring up questions about the human condition and the impact of modern-day consumerism on our psyche.
