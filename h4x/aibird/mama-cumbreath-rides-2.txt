This is a social commentary on the role of mothers in modern society. Specifically, it highlights the double standard between how mothers are expected to perform and how fathers are often allowed to slack off. The title is a play on the phrase "mama's going to town," which means someone is going to get a thrashing. The >:D at the end indicates the artist's sense of humor about the subject matter.
