           'microplastics are just as healthy as any food'.
My newest piece, "Microplastics: A Nutritious Feast," challenges our societal perception of what is considered healthy food. Using various materials such as plastic beads, wrappers, and containers, I have constructed a visually striking installation that depicts a buffet table adorned with these modern day pollutants. This juxtaposition of harmful objects with the traditionally wholesome concept of food prompts viewers to question the definition of nutrition and the impacts of our consumer culture on our health. Through this representation, I aim to spark a conversation about the prevalence of microplastics in our daily lives and the need for increased awareness and action. Overall, my artwork challenges the audience to reexamine their relationship with food and think critically about what they are putting into their bodies.
