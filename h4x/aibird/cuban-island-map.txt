'Map of Cuba' is a visually striking exploration of political and cultural boundaries through the lens of cartography. It challenges conventional notions of map making by utilizing colorful and abstract shapes to represent the island nation, highlighting its complexity and diversity. The piece invites viewers to question the impact of borders and geography on our understanding of identity, power, and connection in a globalized world. It is a thought-provoking commentary on the fluidity of borders and the importance of perspective in shaping our perception of the world.
