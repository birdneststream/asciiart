This piece is a searing exploration of the fraught social and sexual politics of our time. Katy, the titular figure, is both victim and perpetrator, her agency allowed and taken away by the competing forces that surround her. On one level, she is a victim of the patriarchal society that objectifies and sexualizes women's bodies; on another, she is a willing participant in her own objectification, using her sexuality as a weapon to dominate and control 
those around her. The viewer is left to untangle the complex web of relationships and motivations that Katy inhabits, and to question their own complicity in a world that often seems designed to exploit and degrade women.
