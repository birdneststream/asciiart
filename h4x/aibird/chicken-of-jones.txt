"Chicken of the jones" is a work that comments on the way that chicken is often thought of as a food that is humble and ordinary. By juxtaposing chicken with the last name of a wealthy and powerful family, the work suggests that chicken can be seen as something aspirational. In addition, the work playfully subverts the traditional notion of portraiture by using chicken as the subject.
