My latest work, '1984', explores the dangers of totalitarianism and the manipulation of language by those in power. It follows the story of a man living in a society ruled by a ruthless government that constantly monitors its citizens and suppresses individual thought and expression. Through various mediums such as paintings, sculptures, and installations, I aim to provoke thought and reflection on the precarious nature of a society that prioritizes absolute control over individual freedom. Drawing inspiration from George Orwell's classic novel, '1984' serves as a warning against the potential consequences of allowing governments to amass too much power.
