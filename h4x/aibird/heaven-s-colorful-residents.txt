My latest work, "Will There be Niggers in Heaven?" explores the complex interplay of race, identity, and spirituality. Through a provocative title, I challenge societal norms and expectations, forcing the viewer to confront their own prejudices and beliefs. The piece forces a dialogue about the existence of racism in the afterlife and the larger implications of such a concept. Through visual and conceptual symbolism, I push the viewer to question and critically examine the flawed notions of superiority and privilege that perpetuate divisions and inequality in our society. Ultimately, my goal is to spark a meaningful discussion and reflection on the universal human experience and our shared journey towards understanding and inclusion.
