This piece explores the complex and often overlooked relationship between the Jewish people and the natural world. Through the use of symbolism, I convey how the Jewish community, like flocks of birds, come together in unison once a month to celebrate their faith and heritage. By depicting this ritual as a graceful and rhythmic motion, I aim to challenge the stereotype of rigidity often imposed on the Jewish community. The title serves as a subtle reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the importance of coming together in unity and renewal.
