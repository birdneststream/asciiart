"False Victimhood" is a thought-provoking commentary on the societal tendency to project oneself as a victim without genuine cause. Through abstract imagery and distorted symbolism, I challenge the viewer to question their own perception of victimhood and explore the possible manipulation and exploitation within our victim-focused culture. It serves as a reminder that true empathy and compassion should not be taken for granted, and that false victimhood only perpetuates societal divisions.
