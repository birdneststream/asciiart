November marks 0Odd15's anniversary of getting involved in the underground PC
artscene, so it's really a bit weird to see us sharing art from relatively
early in his ANSI career that we've kept bottled up until a thematically
relevant opportunity came up.  But it's an interesting vignette shedding
light on the peculiar way we've been doing things around here.  We've
discussed at some length on our Discord how we are planning on pursuing a new
direction in 2021, with less of a focus on our (14unexpectedly successful15) run
of themed artpacks released on a monthly schedule, and more of a focus on
"when it's ready" projects created by actually-active contributors to our
community (14vs. what we have head-scratchingly characterised as15 "the
slushpile" 14-- work that isn't exclusive to us or necessarily made with our15
14audiences in mind, but that we have been given authorization to signal boost15
14and recirculate.15)  It's perhaps a good thing that we've been looking into
this pivot before having it rudely thrust upon us, since judging by how many
of our nominally-participating artists' portfolios have either been exhausted
or actively closed up over the course of 2020, it's coming upon us whether we
want it or not!  Like the old expression has it: 0change is inevitable; growth15
0is optional15.  In the meantime, the last of that wonderful slush is on its way
to your screens, but within a few months we should be done with it forever!

another pack or two coming up to round out the year, definitely some coming
up in the Spring, and don't forget -- the next 0Blender15 competition will be

but other creative works in other mediums such as games and the
